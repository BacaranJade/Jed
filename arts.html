Larry Alcala (National Artist for Visual Arts)

Biography:


Full Name: Lauro “Larry” Zarate Alcala

Birthdate: August 18, 1926

Death: June 24, 2002


Background:

Born in Daraga, Albay, Larry Alcala studied Fine Arts at the University of the Philippines. He became one of the most famous Filipino cartoonists, starting his career with Siopawman in 1946.


Influences: His works were influenced by Filipino culture, humor, and social satire.


Artistic Contributions:


Notable Works:


Slice of Life:

A comic strip that humorously depicted everyday Filipino life.


Kalabog en Bosyo and Asiong Aksaya:

Comedic comic strips with strong cultural relevance.


Artistic Style: Alcala’s style was characterized by clean lines, humor, and relatable everyday situations. His work, full of cultural references, helped Filipinos see the humor in their day-to-day lives.


Impact: He made Filipino culture accessible to all, blending art with humor. His comic strips are still considered classics in Philippine cartoon history. Legacy.

Uwang Ahadas (GAMABA Artist)

Biography:


Full Name: Haji Uwang Ahadas

Birthdate: February 15, 1945

Background:

Born in Lamitan, Basilan, Haji Uwang Ahadas is a master of Yakan traditional music. He learned to play various traditional instruments such as the kulingtangan, agung, and gabbang. Despite developing impaired vision at a young age, Ahadas continued to master these instruments, driven by his commitment to preserving the rich musical traditions of the Yakan people.


Influences: His works were influenced by Filipino culture, humor, and social satire.


Artistic Contributions:


Notable Works:


Ahadas is renowned for his exceptional skill with traditional Yakan instruments. He not only performed but also shared his knowledge with others, teaching younger generations to ensure these traditions would not fade.


Artistic Style: His mastery of traditional Yakan instruments reflects the deep cultural heritage of the Yakan community, contributing to the preservation of their musical practices.


Cultural Impact & Legacy: Haji Uwang Ahadas was awarded the Gawad sa Manlilikha ng Bayan (GAMABA) in 2000 for his role in keeping the Yakan musical traditions alive. His dedication to teaching and performing has ensured the survival of these cultural practices for future generations.

Federico Caballero (GAMABA Artist)

Biography:


Birthdate: December 23, 1938.


Background:


Federico Caballero hails from the Sulod-Bukidnon people of Panay. Growing up, he learned the Sugidanon epics from his mother and elders in his community. Despite the modern era, he continued to practice and document these oral traditions


Influences: His commitment to preserving ancient epics was rooted in his deep respect for his ancestors and cultural heritage.


Artistic Contributions:


Notable Works:


Caballero is known for his mastery of chanting the Sugidanon epics, which narrate the lives of heroes from Panay.


Artistic Style: His chanting style reflects an ancient oral tradition, preserving a vital link to the cultural heritage of the Visayas.


Cultural Impact & Legacy:He was awarded the GAMABA in 2000 for his efforts to preserve the Sugidanon and for keeping the oral tradition alive.


Why Recognized: His dedication to cultural preservation has allowed the epics to continue being a part of the Sulod-Bukidnon identity.
